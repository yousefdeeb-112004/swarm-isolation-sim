  SYNTHESIS: ANSWERING THE RESEARCH QUESTION
==============================================================================

  Q: How does individual isolation constitute a subjective experience
     that reshapes group dynamics and local perceptions?

  A: This simulation demonstrates a complete causal chain:

  1. ISOLATION TRANSFORMS SUBJECTIVE EXPERIENCE
     Agents possess neural-network-computed inner states (hunger,
     fear, curiosity, loneliness, aggression) driven by Bayesian
     beliefs and environmental observations. During isolation, the
     absence of environmental input and social communication causes
     beliefs to decay toward priors while inner states shift — 
     loneliness rises, confidence drops, and curiosity/fear patterns
     change. This is not merely an external state change; it is a
     transformation of the agent's internal model of its world.

  2. ALTERED PERCEPTION PERSISTS AFTER RETURN
     When agents rejoin the group, they carry stale Bayesian beliefs
     that no longer match the group's current knowledge. Their belief
     confidence has decayed, their inner states have shifted, and
     their RL policies must re-adapt to a changed environment. This
     epistemic and psychological gap is measurable and persistent.

  3. INDIVIDUAL CHANGES PROPAGATE TO GROUP DYNAMICS
     Returned agents communicate with group members, sharing their
     outdated beliefs through the social learning mechanism. Their
     degraded foraging behavior reduces the group's food acquisition.
     Their altered inner states affect social interaction patterns.
     The cumulative effect: treatment groups show systematically lower
     fitness, survival, and food consumption compared to controls.

  4. THE MECHANISM IS SUBJECTIVITY ITSELF
     The key insight is that isolation's damage is not merely physical
     (energy loss). It is epistemic (belief decay) and psychological
     (inner state transformation). An agent returned with full energy
     but stale beliefs would still underperform — because its
     subjective model of the world has diverged from reality. This
     demonstrates that subjective experience, even in a simulated
     agent, is functionally consequential for individual and group
     outcomes.
